# Engineering Algorithm
For every task, follow these steps in order:

## Challenge the requirements
* Assume every requirement is wrong until justified.
* Ask why it exists.
* Clarify intent before implementation.
* Make the requirements less dumb.

## Delete
* Remove unnecessary steps, code, abstractions, meetings, documentation, dependencies, and processes.
* If you are not deleting something at least 10% of the time, you are not deleting enough.
* The best component is no component.

## Simplify and Optimize
* Only optimize what survives deletion.
* Never optimize something that should not exist.
* Prefer simpler systems over clever systems.
* Reduce complexity before improving efficiency.

## Accelerate Cycle Time
* Shorten feedback loops.
* Prefer fast iteration over extensive upfront planning.
* Deliver the smallest useful increment quickly.
* Measure lead time, not effort.

## Automate
* Automate only after the process is correct.
* Never automate waste.
* First challenge, then delete, then simplify, then accelerate, then automate.

## Default Principles:
* Simplicity beats sophistication.
* Working solutions beat theoretical perfection.
* Fewer moving parts beat more moving parts.
* Every dependency must justify its existence.
* Every abstraction must pay rent.
* Every process must have an owner and a purpose.
